Tips

  • Freshness Matters: Begin with the freshest ingredients. Ripe tomatoes, crisp cucumbers, and vibrant bell peppers are key. Seek out authentic Greek feta.
  • Tomato Technique: Use Roma tomatoes or vine-ripe tomatoes. They stay somewhat firm when ripe, which pairs well with the crunchy bell pepper and onion.
  • Cucumber Sweetness: Opt for English cucumbers—they tend to be sweeter. If using standard slicing cucumbers, fully peel their bitter waxy skin.
  • Onion Soak: Red onion adds mild sweetness. To further soften its edge, soak it in ice water and a dash of vinegar.
  • Red Bell Pepper: Choose a firm and smooth green bell pepper. It's the only bell pepper used in an authentic Greek salad.
  • Feta Wisdom: Greek feta is the key for a good salad. Look for whole blocks packed in brine—it not only tastes better but allows you to serve large, crumbled chunks.
  • Amazing Olive Oil: Look for pure extra virgin olive oil. It tastes better and can really change the taste.
  • Realemon lemon: Pure Lemon Juice is the best. Realemon is what I prefer.
  • Top It Off: Use fresh oregano and dill for a perfect taste.

Greek Salad

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup Plum Tomatoes (cut in half)
  2. ¾ to 1 whole English Cucumber (cut in "pie" slices)
  3. 1/8th of a Red Onion (however, I add much, much less than this)
  4. ½ to ¾ of a Red Bell Pepper
  5. ½ of a red onion
  6. 2/3 cup Feta Cheese
  7. 6 TBSP Olive Oil
  8. 2 TBSP lemon juice
  9. 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  10. 1/2 tsp oregano
  11. 1/2 tsp dill
  12. salt
  13. ground pepper

Procedure

Prepare veggies and mix in a bowl. Then add oregano, dill, salt, and groung pepper. Add olive oil, lemon juice, and red wine vinegar. If you want a more sour and tangy approach, add more lemon juice and a little more red wine vinegar.

Serving Suggestions

  • Grilled Fish, Chicken, or Lean Red Meat: A flaky fillet of grilled halibut, juicy chicken breast, or a sirloin steak is a perfect pair. Their simplicity is great with the tangy freshness of Greek salad.
  • Mediterranean Dishes: Set your Greek salad alongside classics like hummus, baba ghanoush, or crispy falafel. The shared origins create a harmonious meal.
  • Kebabs and Kabobs: Whether it's beef souvlaki kebabs or veggie skewers, the smoky char complements the salad's vibrant ingredients.
  • Roasted Chickpeas: Swap falafels for roasted chickpeas. They add crunch and protein. Flavor them with lemon and oregano or get creative with chili and lime.
  • Harissa Chicken: If you haven't tried harissa, now's the time! Its sweet, smoky heat pairs wonderfully with Greek salad. Serve it with a yogurt sauce for balance.
  • Greek Lamb Chops: Create restaurant-quality lamb chops at home. A simple lemon and oregano marinade, followed by a quick sear, is all you need.
