Amazing Greek Salad

By Caleb Levesque
Jump To Recipe For Serving

Our Greek salad isn't steeped in ancient myths, but it carries the warmth of family gatherings and shared laughter. And now you can cook this delicasy.

Ingredients We Love

  1. Sun-Ripened Tomatoes: These tomatoes, kissed by the sun, offer a burst of sweetness. Their vibrant red hue beckons you to take a bite.
  2. Cucumbers: Sliced with care, our English cucumbers bring a cool crunch. They're like whispers of summer in every forkful.
  3. Red Onion Harmony: Thinly sliced red onion adds a gentle kick. It's the harmony in our salad symphony—a nod to balance.
  4. Feta, Our Culinary Anchor: Our feta, crumbled and creamy, is the heartbeat of our salad. It's not just cheese; it's a burst of flavor.

The Lemon and Red Wine Vinegar Tango

Our dressing is a dance—a twirl of lemon and a dip of red wine vinegar.
The lemon brings zest, while the vinegar adds depth. Together, they waltz across the greens.

Why Our Greek Salad Is Special

  • Love Passed Down: Whether it's a weeknight dinner or a Sunday feast, this salad carries the love of those who've stood at our stove.
  • Simple Pleasures: No need for elaborate techniques. Just honest ingredients, tossed with care.
  • Moments Shared: As you savor each bite, remember the stories swapped over this very bowl.
So, whether you're hosting a potluck or enjoying a solo meal, know that our family Greek salad is more than food—it's a memory of love.

Tips

  • Freshness Matters: Begin with the freshest ingredients. Ripe tomatoes, crisp cucumbers, and vibrant bell peppers are key. Seek out quality Kalamata olives and authentic Greek feta.
  • Tomato Technique: Use Roma tomatoes or vine-ripe tomatoes. They stay somewhat firm when ripe, which pairs well with the crunchy bell pepper and onion.
  • Cucumber Sweetness: Opt for English cucumbers—they tend to be sweeter. If using standard slicing cucumbers, fully peel their bitter waxy skin.
  • Onion Soak: Red onion adds mild sweetness. To further soften its edge, soak it in ice water and a dash of vinegar.
  • Briny Olives: Kalamata olives are ideal for a traditional Greek salad. However, any high-quality olive you have on hand will work.
  • Green Bell Pepper: Choose a firm and smooth green bell pepper. It's the only bell pepper used in an authentic Greek salad.
  • Feta Wisdom: Greek sheep's milk feta is best. Look for whole blocks packed in brine—it not only tastes better but allows you to serve large, crumbled chunks.

Serving Suggestions

  • Grilled Fish, Chicken, or Lean Red Meat: Picture a flaky fillet of grilled halibut, juicy chicken breast, or a sirloin steak. Their simplicity pairs beautifully with the tangy freshness of Greek salad.
  • Mediterranean Dishes: Set your Greek salad alongside classics like hummus, baba ghanoush, or crispy falafel. The shared origins create a harmonious meal.
  • Kebabs and Kabobs: Whether it's beef souvlaki kebabs or veggie skewers, the smoky char complements the salad's vibrant ingredients.
  • Roasted Chickpeas: Swap falafels for roasted chickpeas. They add crunch and protein. Flavor them with lemon and oregano or get creative with chili and lime.
  • Harissa Chicken: If you haven't tried harissa, now's the time! Its sweet, smoky heat pairs wonderfully with Greek salad. Serve it with a yogurt sauce for balance.
  • Greek Lamb Chops: Create restaurant-quality lamb chops at home. A simple lemon and oregano marinade, followed by a quick sear, is all you need.
    • Remember, your Greek salad isn't just a side—it's a celebration of flavors.
